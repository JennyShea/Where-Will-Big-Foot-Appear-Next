Our team decided to investigate the places that Bigfoots are often sighted to see if we could determine what factors contribute to a sighting and if we could use that information to predict where future sightings were likely to occur.

Our Factors:

Next, given that the majority of Bigfoot sightings occur in the wilderness, we decided to consider why those who encountered him were out in the woods. Our intuition lead us to three things- population growth, camping and murder. We further wondered, those bigfoot sighters, are they high? To answer those questions, we pulled dataset csvs from the US Census Bureau, US Campgrounds.info, The National Study on Drug Use and Health and the Bigfoot Researchers Organization , loaded them into juypter notebooks and standardized them to group by state and by the years from 2001-2020, with a focus on the 2020 data.

Machine Learning:

The goal of our machine learning model was to see if we could create a model that would be able to predict the number of sightings per state dependent on some key information on each of the states. We looked at 9 different factors. These factors were illicit drug use, alcohol use, substance use disorder, missing people per 100,000 residents, homicide rate, number of campgrounds, and state. We used a supervised machine learning multiple regression model. For our dependent variable we did number of sightings per state and our independent variables were our 8 different factors. We split our data into test and train data then ran our though our model. We received an r-square score of 0.085. This indicates to us that there are far more factors that contribute to the sightings than just the factors that have been added to our machine learning. Other factors that in the future we could take into consideration such as terrain, income, and other wildlife sightings.

Conclusion: Washington!

Looking through all of our Analysis we believe that the best place to see bigfoot is in Washington state.Washington has the highest number of sightings over time.The number of sightings also has a high correlation with our other factors seeming to indicate that they have some bearing as to why there are so many sightings.
